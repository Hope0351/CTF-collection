# Breaking Barriers: Hacking RFID Cards [Beginner Level]

Ever wondered how easy it is to hack an RFID card? In this blog, I’ll show you the basics of RFID technology and how to clone a card using tools like Proxmark3 and MIFARE magic cards. While it may seem harmless, the potential for misuse is bigger than you think.

Let’s assume you’re new to RFID, so we will start with the basics.

## Introduction to RFID technology:

RFID, or Radio Frequency Identification, is a technology that uses radio waves to read and capture information stored on a chip. To keep things simple, RFID systems consist of two primary components:

A) Reader (The Scanner)

B) Card (The Tag)

When the card/tag is near the reader/scanner, the reader/scanner picks up the card/tag’s unique information through electromagnetic waves.

While RFID technology offers convenience, it also comes with various vulnerabilities. Some common security risks include:

- Replay Attacks: This involves capturing and reusing signals to gain unauthorized access.

- Cloning: This refers to copying RFID data to create a duplicate tag.

- Brute Force Attacks: This is the process of attempting multiple combinations to guess the data stored on a tag.

In this blog, we will focus specifically on cloning.
