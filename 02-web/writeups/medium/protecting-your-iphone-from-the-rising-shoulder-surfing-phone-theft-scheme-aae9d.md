# :globe_with_meridians: Protecting Your iPhone from the Rising “Shoulder Surfing” Phone Theft Scheme

Here are 5 actions that you can take now to minimize the damage of this scheme —

## 1. Block access to modifying your iCloud account using Screen Time.


Instead of thieves locking you out of your iCloud, you can lock them out using this Screen Time hack. In your iPhone, go to* Screen Time* (through search or settings) > turn on *Content & Privacy Restrictions *> Don’t Allow *Account Changes* > Don’t Allow *Passcode Changes*.


Next, go to *Screen Time* > *Use Screen Time Password* > Create password > Skip Apple ID recovery. Now, a thief cannot make changes to your iCloud account without entering this new password.

## 2. Use a separate password manager.


If you store passwords in iCloud Keychain, browser auto-fill, your notes app or photos, a thief can access all of these passwords with just your phone password. Password managers have a master password to access your passwords, adding an additional layer of security. From a quick Google search, [Bitwarden](https://bitwarden.com/) and [1Password](https://1password.com/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=10693428688&utm_content=479754879319&utm_term=how+to+manage+passwords&gclid=Cj0KCQjwt_qgBhDFARIsABcDjOfOisE-nhnWPyuuyW_CSV9kjJNu9xLZ2zBnLuVqHIf2xQPgfJyddgUaAvawEALw_wcB&gclsrc=aw.ds) seem to be the top password managers. Alternatively, consider a password-protected file to store passwords.

## 3. Backup your phone.


If you have to erase your iPhone remotely to limit access to your phone, make sure that you have a backup of what you wish to keep, such as photos, messages, and contacts. You can do this through iCloud (after step 1, your iCloud account should be more secure) or through another tool.

## 4. Don’t use Face ID / Touch ID to log into apps.


Often times, we use Face ID / Touch ID to quickly log into essential apps, such as finance & banking apps. However, when these log-in methods don’t work, one can always input the phone passcode to continue. Even though biometric methods are more convenient, typing in login information is worth the additional layer of security.


Another less-secure alternative (if you *really* want to keep Face ID / Touch ID), is to use Screen Time with passcode, and limit usage of sensitive apps before having to enter the Screen Time passcode.

## 5. Delete sensitive photos and information from your phone.


In these phone theft schemes, thieves often search for SSNs, personal IDs, and financial information in emails, photos, and messages. It’s important to remove such sensitive information from your phone — for example, pictures of government ID cards, or Google Drive files with your social security number.
