# 😱 XSS, CSRF, SSRF… WTF? Web Vulnerabilities Explained Simply

Ever read a cybersecurity article and thought, *“Wait — what the heck are all these acronyms? Is this a secret hacker language?”* Trust me, you’re not alone.

XSS, CSRF, SSRF — these acronyms aren’t some nerdy spellcasting incantations. They’re sneaky web vulnerabilities hackers exploit every single day. If you’re a developer, security professional, or just someone who’s curious, understanding these threats is your first step to keeping yourself safe online.

Let’s dive in — with fewer acronyms and more relatable analogies.

## 🔥 1. XSS: The Hacker’s Sneaky Mirror Trick

Ever had a friend prank you by replacing your shampoo with hair dye? Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) is kinda like that — but on websites.

XSS occurs when attackers inject malicious scripts (think: JavaScript) into trusted websites you regularly visit. Imagine your favorite shopping site suddenly sending your cookies, passwords, or credit card info straight to hackers. Not cool, right?

Think of XSS as hackers handing you a mirror where you unknowingly spill your own secrets — straight back to them.
