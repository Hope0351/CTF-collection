## Overview

Repositories (or the backends for web platforms) that host always-on challenges designed for self-paced learning.

Practice platforms are the primary on-ramp for new CTF players. picoCTF, pwn.college, HackTheBox, and TryHackMe collectively introduce the majority of new players to the format each year.

## How to Use These Repositories

Practice platforms are best used as **structured learning environments**. They are the primary on-ramp for new CTF players and the most common source of beginner-tier writeups.

The four major practice platforms — picoCTF, pwn.college, HackTheBox, and TryHackMe — collectively introduce the majority of new CTF players to the format each year. Each has a different pedagogical style: picoCTF is gamified and beginner-friendly; pwn.college is structured and academic; HackTheBox is competition-style with progressive difficulty; TryHackMe is browser-based and accessible.
