# Aggregators / Archives

> Part of **CTF-collection** — see [`30-archetypes/README.md`](README.md) for the full archetype index.

## Overview

Repositories that collect and organize CTF challenges and/or writeups from many events. Unlike awesome-lists (which link out), aggregators typically host the actual writeup content or challenge files locally.

Aggregators provide a stable long-term archive even if original event websites disappear. `sajjadium/ctf-archives` is the closest thing to a comprehensive CTF challenge corpus and is the natural starting point for large-scale analysis.

## How to Use These Repositories

Aggregators are best used for **corpus-scale research**. If you need to study many CTF challenges at once (e.g., for vulnerability-classification research, or for training an automated CTF-solving agent), the aggregators are the natural starting point.

`sajjadium/ctf-archives` is the closest thing to a comprehensive CTF challenge corpus; `momenbasel/htb-writeups` is uniquely suited to graph-based analysis of attack-path patterns thanks to its interactive knowledge graph.
